Nothing against a Starbucks when you're in an airport. But Toronto has an independent coffee scene that genuinely competes with any city in North America, and once you find your spots, there's no going back.

These are five of the best local cafes in the city — spread across neighbourhoods, different vibes, all worth knowing.

## [Moments](/cafes/moments) — Downtown Toronto

![Moments cafe interior in Downtown Toronto](/photos/moments.jpg)

One of the highest-rated cafes in the city with a loyal following that shows up daily. Moments is warm, intimate, and does the kind of coffee that makes you slow down. If you're downtown and want somewhere that feels nothing like a chain, this is the answer.

**Best for:** Slowing down, a proper coffee break
**Order:** Latte or filter coffee

---

## [Fungo Cafe](/cafes/fungo-cafe) — King West

![Fungo Cafe espresso bar on King West, Toronto](/photos/fungo-cafe.jpg)

One of the top-rated cafes in the west end and a strong argument for why independent espresso bars exist. Fungo is focused, precise, and the kind of place that reminds you what good coffee actually tastes like. Small room, big reputation.

**Best for:** Espresso, quick solo visits
**Order:** Espresso or cortado

---

## [Crimson Teas](/cafes/crimson-teas) — Chinatown

![Crimson Teas in Chinatown, Toronto](/photos/crimson-teas.jpg)

Over 500 reviews and one of the most-loved spots in Chinatown. Crimson Teas does specialty drinks with real care, and the following it's built reflects that. A strong alternative for anyone who usually reaches for a Starbucks seasonal drink out of habit.

**Best for:** Specialty drinks, Chinatown visits
**Order:** Whatever's seasonal

---

## [Ninetails Coffee](/cafes/ninetails-coffee-bloor) — The Annex

![Ninetails Coffee on Bloor Street in The Annex, Toronto](/photos/ninetails-coffee-bloor.jpg)

Ninetails has built a real following in the Annex with great coffee and a space that feels considered without being cold. One of those neighbourhood spots that raises the bar for everyone around it. Worth a detour if you're not already nearby.

**Best for:** Annex mornings, specialty coffee lovers
**Order:** Latte or pour-over

---

## [FIKA Cafe](/cafes/fika-cafe) — Kensington Market

![Interior of FIKA Cafe in Kensington Market, Toronto](/photos/fika-cafe.jpg)

FIKA is one of the most-reviewed independent cafes in Toronto and consistently delivers. Swedish-inspired, well-designed, and reliably good. If you've never been to Kensington Market, FIKA is a good reason to go. If you have, you already know.

**Best for:** Weekend mornings, pastries, lingering
**Order:** Cardamom latte or drip coffee
