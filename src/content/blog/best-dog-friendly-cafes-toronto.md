Finding a dog-friendly cafe in Toronto is harder than it sounds. Most places follow the same playbook: dogs can technically sit with you on the patio, weather permitting, but the welcome stops there. No water bowl, no acknowledgment, no special consideration. You're on borrowed time until someone complains or the patio fills up with people who didn't sign up to share the space with a 60-pound Labrador.

The cafes below are different. These are places where the welcome is genuine — backed up by reviews from people who actually showed up with their dogs and had a good experience. One place actively keeps dog treats behind the counter. Another has reviewers specifically noting they chose the spot because of the dog-friendly terrace. A third has a review that calls it out by name: "dogs are welcome."

Toronto's dog-owning population is large, and it deserves better than a grudging shrug from the guy clearing tables. These three cafes have earned their recommendation.

## Cafe23 — Queen West

![Cafe23 - hidden garden terrace on Queen West](/photos/cafe23.jpg)

**728 Queen St W | Mon–Fri 7am–7pm, Sat–Sun 8am–7pm**

[Cafe23](/cafes/cafe23) is one of those cafes that photographs better than you expect, and then turns out to be better in person than in the photos. The address is on Queen West, but the heart of the place is in the back — a multi-level garden terrace that sits tucked behind the main building, removed enough from the street that it barely feels like you're in the city.

That terrace is where you want to be with your dog. A reviewer specifically noted: *"We walked there because our dog was allowed on their back terrace, which is absolutely lovely."* That kind of deliberate trip — where the terrace was the deciding factor — says something. They didn't stumble in. They came because of it.

The coffee and tea menu is thoughtful, with good flat whites and an interesting selection beyond espresso basics. The pastries are worth ordering — fresh and well-executed. If you happen to be a St-Viateur Bagel fan, they carry them here, which is a nice surprise this far from Montreal.

The space itself is densely planted, with greenery throughout both the interior and the outdoor levels. The inside seating is on the snugger side, but that's not where you're headed anyway. The terrace has room to spread out, and the tables have enough space that a dog under the table doesn't turn into a game of Tetris.

With 1,301 reviews and a 4.5-star average, Cafe23 has clearly built a following. The neighbourhood draw helps, but the quality is consistent enough that people keep coming back without needing to be reminded. For dog owners on Queen West, this should be the first stop.

## The Abbott — Liberty Village

![The Abbott - neighbourhood cafe in Liberty Village](/photos/the-abbott.jpg)

**99 Spencer Ave | Daily 7:30am–6pm**

[The Abbott](/cafes/the-abbott) sits on Spencer Avenue at the quieter, residential edge of Liberty Village — not on the main King Street strip, but close enough to everything while feeling more like a neighbourhood spot than a destination cafe. That distinction matters when you have a dog with you. A cafe attached to the neighbourhood tends to be more relaxed about what kind of company walks through the door.

The Abbott earns its place here from one line in a review that's hard to improve on: *"The doggies can get a free treat too."* That's not a vague patio policy or a technically-dogs-are-outside loophole. That's a cafe that keeps treats for your dog. A place that's thought about this, made a decision, and followed through.

It's a $ price point, which is relatively rare for specialty coffee in Toronto. The rating is 4.7 stars across 337 reviews, and the description that comes up repeatedly in reviews is neighbourhood staple — the kind of place people go every week without really thinking about it, which is the highest compliment a local cafe can receive.

The outdoor seating includes red benches on the sidewalk, which in warmer months gives you a proper place to sit with your dog without crowding into a tight patio. The coffee is organic, the baked goods are the sort that reward coming back and working through the options. The owner and staff come up in reviews for being warm and remembered.

For Liberty Village residents with dogs, this is the obvious answer. But it's also worth the trip from other parts of the west end — Spencer Ave is a pleasant walk from the Dufferin street area, and the neighbourhood feel makes it an easy place to spend an hour without feeling like you're in the way. More details on the surrounding area in our [Liberty Village cafe guide](/blog/liberty-village-cafe-guide).

## Café Negin — King West

![Café Negin - cozy dog-welcoming cafe on King West](/photos/caf-negin.jpg)

**787 King St W | Mon–Thu 8am–4pm, Fri 8am–5pm, Sat–Sun 9am–5pm**

[Café Negin](/cafes/caf-negin) changed ownership not long ago, and the new owner brought a different kind of warmth to the space. The review that puts this cafe on this list is specific: *"They have blankets for a cozy vibe, dogs are welcome, and their tables are spacious."* Three details, all meaningful — the blankets suggest they want you to stay, the dog welcome is stated plainly, and spacious tables mean you're not negotiating for space.

The menu leans Turkish-inspired: saffron tea, chocolate croissant, pistachio croissant, and cookies. These aren't items you find everywhere in Toronto, which makes Café Negin worth visiting on its own terms, not just as the dog-friendly option in the area. The food quality comes up consistently in reviews. The chai latte also appears frequently with enthusiasm.

Another reviewer called it *"a lovely lady"* when describing the owner being present on their visit and offering warm soup to customers on a cold day. That's the sort of detail that doesn't make it into a Yelp rubric, but it's the thing that determines whether a place feels like a business or a neighbourhood anchor.

With 892 reviews at 4.2 stars, it has the review volume that comes from a genuinely active cafe, not a sleeper that only locals know about. The outdoor seating means you have somewhere to settle with your dog when the weather allows. The morning hours make it a realistic option before a dog walk, not just after.

If you're on King West with your dog and want something better than takeaway coffee from a chain, Café Negin is the right call. And if you're exploring the broader strip, the [King West cafe guide](/blog/king-west-cafe-guide) covers other options in the area.

## What to Know Before You Go

A few practical notes for bringing your dog to any Toronto cafe:

**Call ahead in summer.** Patio policies can shift during peak season when cafes get busier and more crowded. What's welcoming in February might require a quick conversation in July. A short phone call or DM usually gets a clear answer.

**Stay on the patio.** Ontario's health regulations generally prohibit dogs inside food-service spaces (with the exception of certified service animals). Even at the most welcoming cafes, this means your dog stays outside. Plan for the patio specifically, not a seat by the window.

**Bring water.** Most cafes won't have a bowl ready, and your dog will need it, especially in warmer months. A collapsible travel bowl takes up almost no space and removes the need to ask.

**Go during off-peak hours.** Weekday mornings are easier for dog owners than Saturday afternoons. The patio is less crowded, the staff have more bandwidth to be accommodating, and your dog has more room to settle without being in the way of passing tables.

**Respect the space.** Even at genuinely dog-friendly spots, other customers didn't necessarily sign up for an interaction. Keep your dog close, clean up immediately if needed, and tip well. These places are making a choice to welcome dogs, and making it easy for them to keep doing so is on you.
