Yorkville has a reputation for being polished and expensive, and some of its cafes fit that description perfectly. But the neighbourhood also hides genuinely great independent spots that don't trade on the postcode alone. The coffee scene here is more varied than people expect.

## How to spend a morning in Yorkville

Start at [Goldstruck](/cafes/goldstruck-coffee) or [Coco](/cafes/coco-espresso-bar) when they open at 7am for an early coffee before the streets fill up. Walk Cumberland or Yorkville Avenue while it's quiet. Stop into [Amadeus Patisserie](/cafes/amadeus-patisserie) at 7:30am for something to eat. If you want the best cup in the neighbourhood, [Sip Sip](/cafes/sip-sip) opens at 9am and it's worth the wait. For evenings, [Coco](/cafes/coco-espresso-bar) stays open until 11pm and [ZAZA](/cafes/zaza-espresso-bar) until 10pm: two of the latest closes in the city.

Here's what each one is actually like.

---

## [Sip Sip](/cafes/sip-sip): The hidden one

![Sip Sip cafe interior in Yorkville, Toronto](/photos/sip-sip.jpg)

The highest-rated cafe in the neighbourhood and the least obvious about it. Sip Sip has a 4.9★ rating and the kind of loyal regulars who don't advertise the place. Opens at 9am and stays until 9pm, one of the longer windows on the strip. Go before it gets busy.

**Best for:** The best coffee in Yorkville, unhurried visits, evenings
**Order:** Whatever they recommend that day

---

## [EPOS Cafe & Restaurant](/cafes/epos-cafe-restaurant): The all-day one

![EPOS Cafe and Restaurant in Yorkville, Toronto](/photos/epos-cafe-restaurant.jpg)

A Yorkville all-day spot rated 4.8★ that handles breakfast and lunch as confidently as it handles espresso. Opens at 8am, closes at 4pm. The food is the draw but the coffee holds up. A good option when you want more than a quick stop.

**Best for:** Breakfast, long mornings, coffee and a proper meal
**Order:** Espresso and whatever is on the menu

---

## [Aeset Coffee Co.](/cafes/aeset-coffee-co): The specialty one

![Aeset Coffee Co. cafe in Yorkville, Toronto](/photos/aeset-coffee-co.jpg)

A quiet Yorkville gem with a serious approach to sourcing and brewing. Small, focused, and the kind of place that rewards regulars. Rated 4.6★ across 316 reviews. Opens at 8am, closes at 6pm. One of the best spots in the neighbourhood if you care about what's in the cup.

**Best for:** Specialty coffee, solo visits, afternoon sessions
**Order:** Pour-over or espresso

---

## [Goldstruck Coffee](/cafes/goldstruck-coffee): The neighbourhood one

![Goldstruck Coffee bright interior in Yorkville, Toronto](/photos/goldstruck-coffee.jpg)

Bright, well-designed, and the most-reviewed cafe in Yorkville with over 1,900 Google ratings at 4.5★. Goldstruck is the kind of place that works equally well for a quick coffee and a two-hour sit. Opens at 7am, closes at 8pm: solid hours for a neighbourhood that runs late.

**Best for:** Morning or afternoon visits, reliable quality, working
**Order:** Latte or filter coffee

---

## [Amadeus Patisserie](/cafes/amadeus-patisserie): The bakery

![Amadeus Patisserie baked goods and coffee in Yorkville, Toronto](/photos/amadeus-patisserie.jpg)

A Yorkville patisserie with standout baked goods and espresso that holds its own. Opens at 7:30am, closes at 7:30pm. The pastries are the real reason to go but the coffee is good enough that you won't feel like you compromised. Get there early on weekends for the best selection.

**Best for:** Coffee and something to eat, morning visits, weekends
**Order:** Espresso and a pastry

---

## [LADURÉE Toronto](/cafes/ladur-e-toronto-yorkville-caf): The Parisian one

![LADURÉE Toronto Yorkville patisserie cafe interior](/photos/ladur-e-toronto-yorkville-caf.jpg)

The Toronto outpost of the legendary Parisian patisserie. LADURÉE is a genuinely special visit, with macarons, pastries, and a room that earns its place in Yorkville's high-end stretch. Opens at 8am, stays until 9pm. Not an everyday cafe but the right choice for a specific occasion.

**Best for:** A special visit, coffee and macarons, afternoon treats
**Order:** Macaron and a coffee

---

## [ZAZA Espresso Bar](/cafes/zaza-espresso-bar): The late one

![ZAZA Espresso Bar compact espresso bar in Yorkville, Toronto](/photos/zaza-espresso-bar.jpg)

A compact espresso bar open until 10pm, one of the later closes in the neighbourhood. ZAZA punches above its size with well-made espresso and a no-fuss atmosphere. Opens at 7am. The best option when you need coffee after most of Yorkville has closed down for the evening.

**Best for:** Evening coffee, quick espresso stop, late afternoons
**Order:** Espresso or cortado

---

## [Coco Espresso Bar](/cafes/coco-espresso-bar): The latest one

![Coco Espresso Bar warm interior in Yorkville, Toronto](/photos/coco-espresso-bar.jpg)

Open until 11pm on weekdays and with nearly 1,000 Google reviews at 4.4★, Coco is Yorkville's best late-night coffee option by a significant margin. The espresso is strong, the room is comfortable, and the hours make it genuinely useful. Opens at 7am. Reliable from morning through to close.

**Best for:** Late evenings, long visits, reliable coffee any time of day
**Order:** Espresso or latte

---

Yorkville has more going on than the neighbourhood's reputation suggests. Whether you're after the best single cup in the city or somewhere to sit until 11pm with a good espresso, the options are here.
