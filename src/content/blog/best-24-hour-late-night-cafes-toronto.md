Toronto's cafe scene has a hard-close problem. Most specialty coffee shops are done by 5pm, and even the generous ones are out the door by 7 or 8. That leaves an awkward gap for anyone who wants a proper coffee in the evening — not a chain drive-through, not a diner coffee, but an actual good cup in an actual decent space.

True 24-hour independent cafes are essentially non-existent in Toronto. But there is a solid handful of places that take late-night coffee seriously, staying open until 10pm, 11pm, or even midnight on weekends. This guide covers the best of them, with honest notes on which days they're actually worth visiting late.

---

## Nabulu Coffee — Open Until 11pm, Every Night

**Downtown Toronto** · 8am–11pm, seven days a week

![Nabulu Coffee - Downtown Toronto late night cafe](/photos/nabulu-coffee.jpg)

[Nabulu Coffee](/cafes/nabulu-coffee) is the most reliably late option in this list. Every single night of the week, it runs until 11pm — no weekend-only extension, no early Thursday close. That kind of consistency is rare.

The cafe sits on St Joseph St in the Church-Wellesley Village, with outdoor seating and a space that handles the shift from morning coffee crowd to evening wind-down well. The coffee is specialty, taken seriously. Nabulu has real character — it doesn't feel like a chain that stayed open late by accident, but like a place that chose its hours intentionally.

With a 4.6 rating from nearly 500 reviews and outdoor seating available, it draws a mix of students, post-dinner walkers, and people who simply want a proper coffee at 10pm. If you need a dependable late-night option in the downtown core and don't want to think too hard about what day it is, this is your best bet.

## Coco Espresso Bar — Until Midnight on Weekends

**Yorkville** · Mon–Thu 7am–11pm · Fri–Sat 7am–12am · Sun 8am–11pm

![Coco Espresso Bar - Yorkville espresso bar open late](/photos/coco-espresso-bar.jpg)

[Coco Espresso Bar](/cafes/coco-espresso-bar) is the only spot in this list that pushes past midnight — and it does so on Fridays and Saturdays when it counts most. The rest of the week it closes at 11pm, making it one of the most consistently late cafes in the city overall.

It's on Bellair St in Yorkville, which means the room has a certain polish to it. Espresso is the focus, and the standard is high. This isn't a place to linger for hours with a laptop — it's better suited to someone who wants a proper espresso at 10:30pm before a night out, or a wind-down coffee after dinner in the neighbourhood.

Outdoor seating and nearly 1,000 reviews at 4.4 stars — it's a known quantity. For more on what Yorkville offers during daylight hours, the [Yorkville cafe guide](/blog/yorkville-cafe-guide) covers the full picture.

## ZAZA Espresso Bar — Late Thursday Through Saturday

**Yorkville** · Mon–Wed 7am–10pm · Thu–Sat 7am–11pm · Sun 7am–10pm

[ZAZA Espresso Bar](/cafes/zaza-espresso-bar) is the compact counterpart to Coco, a few minutes' walk away on Cumberland St. Open at 7am and running late on Thursdays through Saturdays, ZAZA is a quick-moving counter espresso bar with precise technique and a no-nonsense approach.

The space is small, which keeps it lively even with a modest evening crowd. During the week it closes at 10pm — still useful for an after-dinner coffee — and on weekends stretches to 11pm. Outdoor seating is available. If you're spending an evening in Yorkville and want a proper coffee rather than something dessert-style, ZAZA is worth knowing about.

## NEO Coffee Bar King x Spadina — Late on Fridays and Saturdays

**Chinatown** · Mon–Thu 7am–9pm · Fri 7am–11pm · Sat 8am–11pm · Sun 8am–9pm

![NEO Coffee Bar King x Spadina - late night Chinatown coffee](/photos/neo-coffee-bar-king-x-spadina.jpg)

[NEO Coffee Bar King x Spadina](/cafes/neo-coffee-bar-king-x-spadina) is the high-traffic corner location of one of Toronto's most recognizable specialty coffee brands. With over 1,600 reviews at 4.4 stars, it's a proven spot — consistent, busy, and well-run.

The late hours on Fridays and Saturdays (until 11pm) make it worth knowing about for weekend evenings in the King/Spadina corridor. Outdoor seating, good for groups, and it handles volume without sacrificing quality. During the week it closes at 9pm — useful for an evening coffee, not quite late-night.

The Chinatown/Spadina area has a lot going on late into the night. If you want context for the full neighbourhood, the [Chinatown cafe guide](/blog/chinatown-cafe-guide) covers the options.

## I DEAL Coffee & Wine — The Late-Night Ossington Option

**Ossington** · Mon–Wed 7:30am–6pm · Thu 7:30am–8pm · Fri–Sat 7:30am–11pm · Sun 7:30am–6pm

[I DEAL Coffee & Wine](/cafes/i-deal-coffee-wine) does two things well: coffee and wine. For the purposes of this guide, the key detail is the Friday and Saturday schedule — open until 11pm on both nights, which is the longest run on Ossington Ave by a meaningful margin.

The format transitions naturally in the evening. It's a cafe that genuinely functions as a wine bar later in the day, which is how it earns the late hours. Fast wifi, outdoor seating, and a "Laptops OK" tag mean it works well for an afternoon work session. By evening it's more social in character.

At a single dollar sign for price, it's one of the more accessible options in this list. The Ossington strip has its own momentum on weekend nights, and I DEAL fits into that without trying too hard. The [Ossington cafe guide](/blog/ossington-cafe-guide) covers what else the street has to offer.

## El Catrin Destileria — Evenings in the Distillery

**Distillery** · Mon–Thu, Sun 12pm–10pm · Fri–Sat 12pm–11pm

[El Catrin Destileria](/cafes/el-catrin-destileria) is the outlier in this list — a large Mexican restaurant and bar in the Distillery District that also handles coffee well. It opens at noon and closes at 10pm most nights, stretching to 11pm on Fridays and Saturdays.

![El Catrin Destileria - Distillery District evening venue](/photos/el-catrin-destileria.jpg)

With over 9,000 reviews, El Catrin is the most-reviewed spot in this guide by a significant margin. The space is beautiful, the Distillery District setting is distinctive, and the menu runs well beyond coffee. Going specifically for a late-evening coffee here makes most sense as a post-dinner move — it's a full venue, not a sit-down-with-a-book cafe. Reservations accepted, outdoor seating available, good for groups.

For evening context on the area, the [Distillery District cafe guide](/blog/distillery-district-cafe-guide) has the full neighbourhood picture.

## Varda — An Evening Cafe in King West

**King West** · Closed Mon · Tue 4pm–10pm · Wed–Thu 3pm–10pm · Fri–Sun 11am–10pm

[Varda](/cafes/varda) is structured differently from the other entries here. On weekdays it opens in the afternoon and evening — 4pm Tuesday, 3pm Wednesday and Thursday. On weekends it runs a full 11am–10pm. It's built around evening use rather than being an all-day spot that happens to stay open late.

The Australian-style cafe approach means strong, carefully-made flat whites in a room with real design intention. King West is the right neighbourhood for it. The 10pm close gives a solid window for after-dinner coffee without needing to rush. Good for groups, no delivery — it's a sit-in space. The [King West cafe guide](/blog/king-west-cafe-guide) covers the broader neighbourhood.

## Project Seoul — Open Until 10pm, Every Day

**Chinatown** · 11am–10pm, seven days a week

[Project Seoul](/cafes/project-seoul) earns its place here for consistency: 10pm every single day of the week, no exceptions. The Korean-inspired cafe on Spadina Ave in Chinatown has a 4.7 rating from over 1,000 reviews — one of the highest-rated cafes in this guide.

![Project Seoul - Chinatown Korean-inspired cafe open late](/photos/project-seoul.jpg)

The space is considered and aesthetic, tagged "Great for dates" and "Aesthetic," both of which are accurate reads. Coffee is thoughtful, the room is designed with care, and outdoor seating is available. It opens at 11am, so it's not a morning cafe — but for anything from noon to 10pm it's a consistently excellent option. The seven-day uniformity removes any need to check the schedule before going.

## ICHA TEA — Tea and Coffee Until 10pm

**Chinatown** · Mon–Thu 12pm–9pm · Fri 12pm–10pm · Sat 11:30am–10pm · Sun 11:30am–9pm

[ICHA TEA](/cafes/icha-tea) handles both tea and coffee with genuine seriousness on Spadina Ave. It extends to 10pm on Fridays and Saturdays, and 9pm on other days. With 1,257 reviews at 4.5 stars, it's a well-established Chinatown destination.

If you're looking for something beyond a flat white — matcha, traditional teas, specialty tea lattes — ICHA TEA is the most legitimate evening option for that in the city. Opening at noon makes it an afternoon-into-evening place by design. The Friday and Saturday 10pm close is the most useful window for late visitors.

## Roozamoon Cafe — Persian-Inspired, Open Until 10pm on Weekdays

**Downtown Toronto** · Mon–Fri 8am–10pm · Sat–Sun 8am–8pm

[Roozamoon Cafe](/cafes/roozamoon-cafe) has the highest rating of any cafe in this guide at 4.9 from nearly 400 reviews. It sits on Queen St E with Persian-inspired touches and an atmosphere that genuinely sets it apart from typical downtown spots.

The catch: it closes at 8pm on weekends, so the late window is weekday-only. Monday through Friday, 10pm is the close — which actually works well for anyone downtown on a work night who wants something better than a chain at 9pm. No outdoor seating, purely a sit-in experience, which suits the character of the space.

## What to Know Before Going Late

A few practical notes for late-night cafe visits in Toronto:

**Hours vary sharply by day of week.** Most of these spots extend their hours only on Thursdays, Fridays, and Saturdays. Showing up on a Tuesday expecting 11pm service will usually disappoint. Always confirm the specific day before making the trip.

**Weeknight evenings are actually quieter.** If you want a late coffee without competing for a seat, Tuesday or Wednesday evening at Nabulu or Roozamoon will give you more space than a Friday night at any of these places.

**Your best bets on a Friday or Saturday night:** Coco Espresso Bar for Yorkville, I DEAL for Ossington, NEO King x Spadina for the Chinatown/King area, and Project Seoul if you want something open at an exact 10pm close. Nabulu is the one that runs to 11pm regardless of the day.

**Food service ends earlier.** Most of these cafes scale back food options as the evening goes on. If you're specifically going for food, aim to arrive before 8pm.
